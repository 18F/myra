About myRA

The U.S. Department of the Treasury is offering myRA (my Retirement Account), a new type of Roth IRA* that is a simple, safe and affordable way to save for retirement. Individuals can open a myRA account with no start-up cost and contribute every payday. myRA will not lose value and there are no fees to maintain the account. The investment is backed by the United States Treasury. Initially, myRA will be made available via direct deposit through participating employers.

Why was myRA developed?

There are millions of working Americans who either don’t have access to an employer-sponsored retirement plan or lack options to save for retirement. In some cases, they don’t have enough money saved to meet a minimum for opening an investment account.

myRA was developed to remove those barriers and provide a simple, safe and affordable path for people who want to start saving with each paycheck. myRA is not a replacement for a 401(k) or other types of employer-sponsored retirement savings accounts.

Who is myRA for?

myRA is for wage earners who have not started to save for retirement and are looking for a simple, safe and affordable way to do so. It’s a good option for anyone who wants to save for retirement but has no access to an employer-sponsored retirement savings plan.

myRA is for individuals with an annual income of less than $129,000 per year, or for married couples filing jointly with an income less than $191,000 per year.

How does myRA work?

The myRA investment will never lose value – it earns interest at the same variable rate as the government securities fund for federal employees. This fund has had an average annual return of 3.39% over the ten-year period from December 2003 to December 2013.

The maximum contribution to myRA is $5,500 per year (or $6,500 per year for individuals 50 years of age or older at the end of the year), and the account can have a maximum account balance of $15,000, or a balance below the maximum for up to 30 years. At any time, individuals can choose to roll over (transfer) their myRA into a private-sector Roth IRA* where they can continue to grow their savings. The United States Treasury will be providing myRA participants with more information about roll over into the private-sector.

When account holders change jobs, their myRA account stays with them, and they can set up payroll direct deposit through their new employer. Individuals with multiple jobs can use direct deposit from each employer to contribute to a single myRA.

How do employers participate?

myRA is free and it’s easy for employers to make it available to employees. Employers may distribute myRA information to employees, but they don’t administer employee accounts, contribute to them, or match employee contributions. On payday, employers simply send a direct deposit to participating employees’ myRAs.

How do individuals get started?

Individuals will be able to sign up for myRA in three simple steps:

  • 1. Open a myRA account through this website
  • 2. Submit a direct deposit authorization to their employer
  • 3. Manage their account online and track their savings

Contact us

If you have questions about myRA, please contact us at myra@treasury.gov.

*For more information on key Roth IRA features go to myRA.treasury.gov/roth-ira.
