How it works

  • 1. Create an account
  • 2. Set up monthly savings
  • 3. Sit back, relax, and stay in control
  • 4. Take the next step

1. Create an account

Whether or not your employer participates in myRA, your first step is to create an account. Creating an account is easy—it takes just __ minutes. Once myRA is open to the public on December 15, just return here to get started.

2. Set up regular savings

With your account set up, it’s time to start saving money. There’s two ways to save:

  1. 1) If your employer is a myRA partner, just ask them to set aside a portion of your paycheck into your myRA. And if you have multiple jobs, you can have them all contribute to the same account. Instructions for setting up employer paycheck contributions »
  2. 2) If your employer is not a myRA partner, or if you’re self-employed, you can simply tell your bank to deposit a set amount of money regularly from your account into your myRA. Instructions for setting up bank account deposits »

3. Sit back, relax, and stay in control

Because your myRA is receiving automatic savings, all you have to do is watch your money grow. myRA accounts earn the same variable rate as the low-risk investment option offered to federal government employees.

You can login to your myRA account to check your balance and manage account settings just like you can at your own bank. And of course, it is your money, so you’re in control. You can change your contribution as often as your employer will allow, and you can even withdraw your principal (the money you put in, not the interest you earn) whenever you want.

4. Take the next step

You can keep saving money in your myRA for 30 years or until you reach $15,000. Once that happens, you will move the money into a private Roth IRA and will decide how to invest it.
